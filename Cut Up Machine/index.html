Cut up Machine

In an attempt to minimize the influence of conscious control on creativity, Surrealist writers (Andre Breton, Tristan Tzara, Guillaume Apollinaire etc) investigated the technique now known as decoupe (or "cut up") in order to generate literary works. The same approach was later adopted by the American writer William Burroughs who (with Brion Gysin) went on to explore the related "fold-in" approach to automatism. These approaches are especially well suited to poetry

More information on the cut up Machine

In this project the idea of human interaction in creative writing will be challenge, by applying the decoupe technique to a source text. And generating a new piece of literature.

1. Input a text by dragging a file, pasting a text or typing.
2. Click on one of the cut-up by buttons.
3. Your cut up text will be right below.

Cut-up by words: Divides the text into words and then shuffles the words.
Cut-up by sentences: Divides the text into sentences and then shuffles the sentences.
Cut-up by words with paragraphs: Divides the text into words and then shuffles the words but also keeps a paragraph structure.
Cut-up by paragraphs: Divides the text into paragraphs and then shuffles the paragraphs.
